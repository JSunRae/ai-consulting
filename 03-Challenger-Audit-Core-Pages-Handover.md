# Challenger Audit for Core and Legacy Pages Handover

Last updated: 2026-05-12

## Audit Frame

Scoring uses the six Challenger stages on a 1-5 scale:

- 1 = largely absent
- 3 = present but inconsistent
- 5 = strong and commercially useful

Classification options:

- keep
- lightly patch
- rewrite
- demote from conversion path

## Executive Decision

The site is no longer broadly generic. The core problem is now inconsistency.

- The homepage, services page, vendor diligence article, customer-service AI article, and AI cost-reduction article already teach before they sell.
- The contact page still behaves more like an intake utility than a Challenger-controlled next step.
- The blog index still behaves too much like an archive and not enough like a guided teaching path.
- About, resume, and portfolio still carry too much profile, proof, and recruiter energy relative to the operator-advisory offer path.
- Several older high-intent posts still close with workshop or audit language that pulls visitors back into legacy packaging.

The right move is not a full rewrite of everything. It is to protect the strong teaching pages, patch the conversion-path breaks, and demote proof-heavy pages so they support trust without becoming the main commercial story.

## Portfolio Positioning Decision

The site is still slightly too portfolio-heavy because portfolio remains a top-level navigation choice alongside the core commercial pages.

That does not mean proof-of-work should be removed.

Recommended position:

- Keep proof-of-work live as a secondary trust layer.
- Use sanitized commercial case studies as the first proof type.
- Keep personal software builds as production-grade capability proof, not as the main commercial story.
- Route homepage and services traffic primarily toward diagnosis and offer pages, not toward portfolio browsing.

Practical demotion moves:

- Reduce portfolio's role in primary conversion copy.
- Keep portfolio linked from proof sections, service pages, and relevant articles.
- Treat resume and portfolio as validation assets once intent already exists.

## Changes Implemented in This Workstream

- Strengthened contact page framing so the next step starts from the decision problem, not the tool request.
- Added a guided path on the blog index so visitors can enter through the strongest teaching pages instead of browsing passively.
- Replaced legacy workshop and audit CTA language on older articles.
- Updated supporting brief language to canonical offer naming.

## Next Rewrite Order

1. Rewrite about.html around buyer relevance, not biography.
2. Rewrite the two legacy GPT and LLM workflow articles into stronger decision-system teaching pages.
3. Demote resume.html and portfolio.html further in the navigation and conversion model if the next funnel pass confirms they still attract too much primary-path attention.
4. Refresh power-bi-vs-tableau-2024-comparison.html and reducing-report-volume-95-percent-case-study.html only if they are still being used as high-traffic entry pages.

## Final Position

The site should now be treated as a controlled commercial funnel with proof layers around it.

- Core teaching and conversion pages stay in front.
- Diagnostic language stays stronger than capability language.
- Portfolio and resume remain useful, but secondary.
- Educational pages should route visitors toward the checklist, fit call, or diagnostic path instead of older workshop and audit packaging.
