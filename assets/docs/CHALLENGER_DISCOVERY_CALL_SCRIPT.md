# Challenger Discovery Call Checklist

Last updated: 2026-05-12

Use this on the first serious buyer conversation.
The goal is to teach one useful thing, diagnose the real commercial issue, and recommend the smallest serious next step.

## 1. Open

Use one clean opening.

`Thanks for making the time. Before we talk about tools, I want to understand the decision or workflow you are trying to improve and where trust is breaking today.`

Checklist:

- Confirm the buyer's role and why they joined
- Confirm the issue is live, not theoretical
- Set the expectation that the discussion is diagnostic, not a free workshop

## 2. Diagnose The Decision Failure

Capture only what is needed.

- What recurring decision is currently slower, weaker, or less trusted because of this issue?
- What happens commercially when it is not fixed?
- Which area is most affected: forecasting, pricing, margin, CRM, reporting, support workflow, or AI software choice?
- Who needs to trust or use the output?
- What is the team doing today that is not working well enough?
- What timeline matters here?

Operator notes:

- Look for workflow failure, not only reporting symptoms
- Separate buyer pain from vendor/demo language
- Translate vague AI ambition into one decision loop or workflow

## 3. Teach One Useful Insight

Pick one, not several.

Examples:

- `The problem is usually not only the dashboard or AI tool. It is the decision logic and workflow around it.`
- `Before buying AI software, it matters whether you are buying a real system, a retrieval layer, or just an API wrapper.`
- `If the goal is headcount reduction, the real test is usually whether the workflow creates measurable capacity, not whether a pilot looked impressive.`
- `Customer-service automation often fails because the business automates avoidable contact instead of fixing why the contact exists.`

Checklist:

- Insight is relevant to the buyer's pressure
- Insight sharpens the problem definition
- Insight leads naturally to a next diagnostic step

## 4. Quantify The Cost

Use one or two questions only.

- How many hours a month go into reconciliation, review, rework, or cleanup?
- What revenue, margin, service quality, or management time is affected?
- How many avoidable contacts or manual interventions sit inside the workflow?
- Have they modeled production usage cost, not only pilot cost?

## 5. Classify The Conversation

Choose the dominant path.

- `Commercial Analytics Diagnostic Review`
  - problem is real but still fuzzy
  - multiple causes are mixed together
  - buyer needs diagnosis before scope
- `AI Software & Vendor Due Diligence`
  - vendor is already shortlisted
  - architecture, data flow, or build-vs-buy is the live decision
  - buyer needs a buy / pilot / reject view
- `Decision Opportunity Prioritization Sprint`
  - too many candidate use cases
  - leadership needs ranking and sequence
- `Commercial Analytics Foundation Fix`
  - broken KPI logic, CRM quality, reporting trust, or governance is already clear
- `Commercial Workflow Deployment`
  - one workflow is defined tightly enough to build
- `Commercial Team Enablement`
  - the real blocker is capability, governance, or adoption

## 6. Recommend The Next Step

Use consistent commercial language.

- Public first paid step: `Commercial Analytics Diagnostic Review - EUR 950 net`
- Public qualification path: `Book Fit Call`
- Public pricing rule: only the Diagnostic Review is publicly priced in this phase
- Standalone but unpriced publicly: `AI Software & Vendor Due Diligence`

Close with:

`Based on what you described, the sensible next step is [offer]. That gives us [specific outcome] before anyone commits to a larger build or software decision.`
