### What is the most relevant thing you have done for this role?

Prepared answer:

`The most relevant through-line is building trusted decision systems. That includes correcting long-running reporting logic errors, rebuilding interpretation frameworks like Price / Volume / FX, leading customer-level acquisition integration, building executive review narratives, and improving the link between analytics outputs and actual commercial decisions.`

### Tell me about the acquisition integration work.

Prepared answer:

`I was central to integrating data across two acquisitions at customer level. That was not just a technical merge. The acquired businesses operated differently, so I had to coordinate across regions and analysts, match customers using fuzzy logic, reconcile credits and account histories, preserve continuity, and restore clean territory measurement. The purpose was to keep operations stable and ensure the sales force could still perform against trusted numbers.`

### Tell me about a time you fixed broken metrics or reporting logic.

Prepared answer:

`One of the clearest examples was correcting more than 10 years of FX methodology issues and rebuilding the Price / Volume / FX decomposition logic with correct interaction effects. The problem was not just mathematical accuracy. Leadership needed an interpretable story they could trust. I rebuilt the logic and improved the way it was communicated, so performance discussions were based on reality rather than distorted reporting.`

### How do you approach KPI design?

Prepared answer:

`I start from the business decision, not the dashboard. What decision is this KPI supposed to improve, who owns it, what behavior should it drive, what does good versus bad signal look like, and what could distort it? Then I work backwards into metric definitions, grain, data lineage, cadence, and governance.`

### How hands-on are you technically?

Prepared answer:

`Hands-on enough to build, diagnose, and challenge. I work in Python, SQL, Power BI, ML, and governed LLM workflows. I am comfortable being player-coach style, especially where the value comes from connecting technical implementation to operating reality.`

### You come from commercial analytics. How would you handle product analytics?

Prepared answer:

`The domain vocabulary changes, but the leadership problem is familiar: define trusted KPIs, create shared measurement logic, resolve ambiguity in the data, align stakeholders, and establish a cadence where teams can act on the signal. My product-analytics depth is lighter than my commercial-analytics depth, but the system-building discipline transfers well.`

### How do you work with executives?

Prepared answer:

`I translate complex data into decision-ready narratives. Executives usually do not need more data volume. They need a clearer signal, the right causal framing, and confidence in what the numbers mean. A large part of my value is reducing ambiguity while keeping analytical integrity intact.`

### Tell me about a conflict with stakeholders.

Prepared answer:

`The common conflict pattern is when a metric or process is politically convenient but analytically weak. My approach is to separate the personal from the structural problem, show the logic clearly, validate assumptions with the relevant teams, and move the discussion toward what decision quality requires rather than who is attached to the current version.`

### How do you lead without a large formal team?

Prepared answer:

`A lot of analytics leadership is influence leadership. I have led through cross-functional ownership, operating cadence, design of measurement logic, structured problem solving, and coordination across analysts, business leaders, finance, BI, and IT. Where I do have formal team responsibility, I naturally operate in player-coach mode.`

### What would your first 90 days look like?

Prepared answer:

`First I would understand the operating rhythm, core business goals, and the current trust level in the numbers. Then I would identify the few metrics, data flows, and stakeholder interfaces that most affect decision quality. Early wins would likely come from KPI clarification, governance cleanup, reporting simplification, and one or two credibility-restoring fixes that improve confidence quickly.`

Response:
`Yes, my deepest experience is on the commercial side. But the repeated problem I solve is still measurement-system leadership: defining KPIs, establishing trust in the data, improving operating cadence, and making analytics actionable.`

`I would not claim something I have not done. My strength is player-coach leadership, cross-functional influence, and creating the structure and trust that make analytics teams effective.`

`Because I want a role where the mandate explicitly includes broader analytics leadership and decision-system ownership, and where compensation reflects that scope.`

## 8. Questions you should ask them

- `What decisions are currently hardest for your teams to make confidently with the existing analytics setup?`
- `Where is trust in the numbers strongest today, and where is it weakest?`
- `What does success in the first 6 months actually look like?`
- `Is the core challenge more about data foundations, KPI definitions, stakeholder alignment, or execution speed?`
- `How would you describe the current relationship between analytics, finance, product, and commercial leadership?`
- `What would make this role fail?`
- `Where do you want analytics to influence behavior more directly than it does today?`

## 9. Topics to handle carefully

- Do not claim a formal title you do not hold.
- Do not overstate product analytics or large-team leadership depth.
- Do not oversell consulting tenure.
- Do not let the conversation collapse into a pure analyst framing.
- Do not quote vanity portfolio metrics unless they clearly help the point.

## 10. Final reminders before calls

- Lead with scope, not title.
- Keep bringing the conversation back to decision quality, KPI trust, and business outcomes.
- Use exact numbers when you have them.
- Avoid sounding defensive about the current title.
- Make the transition from commercial leadership to analytics leadership sound deliberate and useful.
- Use player-coach language where team-size questions appear.
